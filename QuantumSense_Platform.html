Qubit & superposition

A classical bit is a light switch — off or on. A qubit is a spinning coin that holds both states simultaneously. That is superposition — not a guess, a genuine quantum property.

Written as: |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ where |α|² + |β|² = 1.

The Bloch sphere

Every pure qubit state maps to a unique point on a unit sphere. North pole = |0⟩, south pole = |1⟩, equator = equal superposition. θ tilts north-south. φ rotates east-west. Gates = rotations on the sphere.

Measurement & collapse

Measurement forces the qubit to choose — |0⟩ or |1⟩ — with probabilities |α|² and |β|². The collapse is irreversible. Quantum information is permanently destroyed. One of the most profound facts in physics.

Quantum entanglement

Measuring one entangled qubit instantly determines the other — no matter the distance. Einstein called it "spooky action at a distance."

Quantum gates
X — Pauli-X
Quantum NOT. Flips |0⟩↔|1⟩. 180° rotation around x-axis.
Y — Pauli-Y
180° rotation around y-axis. Flip + complex phase.
Z — Pauli-Z
Phase flip of |1⟩. Probabilities unchanged.
H — Hadamard
Creates 50/50 superposition. The quantum parallelism gate.
S — Phase
Phase rotation by π/2. Equivalent to √Z.
T — π/8
Phase rotation by π/4. Universal computation building block.
Why quantum beats classical

Quantum computers are not faster classical computers. Superposition lets them explore many states simultaneously. Entanglement creates correlations impossible classically. Interference amplifies correct answers. For searching databases (Grover: √N vs N), factoring numbers (Shor: polynomial vs exponential), and simulating molecules, quantum algorithms offer provable exponential speedup.
